Wash Days

Pre Poo

Before washing your hair, it is essential to properly prep your hair. Many individuals tend to overlook this step, but trust me when I say it makes all the difference in the world. When your hair is properly prepped before washing, it ensures your hair does not lose too much moisture throughout the wash day process. Moreover, it makes your hair much more manageable.

As you may know from watching my wash day videos, I tend to use hair treatments such as flaxseed gel, hot oil treatments, and creamy hair masks such as the avocado hair mask before washing my hair. These hair treatments help to soften your hair. This makes your hair more manageable and ensures you do not experience unnecessary hair breakage. The hair becomes softer and more moisturized. This means your hair does not tend to snap or become dry when you begin the wash day process.

Another advantage of prepping your hair before washing it is the fact that it helps to prevent tangles. Tangles can be quite stressful and tedious when washing your hair. When your hair is already moisturized and softened from the hair treatments, the wash day process becomes much smoother. This is not just about adding hair products to your hair. Instead, it is about properly prepping your hair before the wash day process. When your hair is properly prepped before washing, it ensures your hair remains in the best state possible.

Wash

Washing your hair helps in the removal of buildup, dirt, and excessive oil from your scalp. This is essential in helping you maintain a conducive environment for hair growth. A clean scalp will help in the growth of your hair, but at the same time, it will cause you to lose your natural oil if you are not careful.

Hair with an Afro texture is normally prone to dryness since the natural oil does not travel down the hair shaft. This implies that after you have washed your hair, it will be dry and rough. This is why it is essential to be careful when washing your hair and always condition it.

Using a gentle shampoo and concentrating on your scalp instead of your hair will help in reducing the dryness. After you have washed your hair, you will need to apply a conditioner or deep condition it. This will help in restoring moisture, making it easy to comb, and reducing dryness.

This step is crucial since it will help in restoring your hair to its natural state after it has been washed. Taking your time in this step will help in making a big difference in the condition of your hair. Washing and conditioning your hair will help in reducing dryness and breakage, which will help promote the growth of your hair.

Styling Your Hair

Once your hair has been washed, conditioned, and detangled completely, it is then ready for styling. This is the part where you lock in all the moisture you have applied throughout the wash day.

Styling is not just about looking good, but it is also about protecting your hair. This is where you apply leave-in conditioners, oil, or cream to lock in moisture and keep your hair looking great and feeling soft. This is an important step because, without it, your hair will dry out in no time.

When it comes to the style, it is always better to go for those styles that will not manipulate your hair too much. Some of the styles include twisting, braiding, or even leaving your hair natural. This will help you maintain your hair because you will not have to keep restyling it throughout the week. This reduces the chances of breakage.

Once you have styled your hair, you will find it easy to maintain it throughout the week. This is because you will not have to keep touching your hair, which will help keep it moist for a longer period.
