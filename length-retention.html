Length Retention

Breakage
When we say length retention, it’s not about growing your hair; it's about maintaining the length of hair that has already grown from your scalp. While your hair keeps growing from your scalp, you can’t see that it has gotten longer. This is because you aren’t retaining your length. Type 4 hair is naturally fragile because of how coiled its structure is; this makes it easily breakable if not cared for properly.

Keeping a consistent and healthy routine is key to retaining length. These routines include providing your hair with sufficient moisture or ensuring gentle detangling. Minimizing high manipulation hairstyles, working gently with your natural hair, and keeping it moisturized throughout the week can help reduce breakage in your ends or roots.

This can directly connect to keeping a consistent and healthy wash day routine for your hair, preferably weekly, whereby you can use nourishing treatments (such as the ones shown in the videos section) that can help prepare your hair for styles for the whole week and reduce breakage over time.
Low Manipulation and Protective Hairstyles
Always remember that the “so-called ugly” hairstyles are the ones that grow your hair. Protective styling helps reduce how often you touch and style your hair. Not all protective styles are actually protective, so it is important to distinguish between them. Low-manipulation styles include styles such as simple twists or braids, with your natural hair, that don’t cause too much tension on your scalp, which are more beneficial than installing heavy extensions.

These kinds of hairstyles can let your hair and scalp rest from constant tension. When you let your hair be more often, it can retain length better and grow to be healthy over time.

Healthy hair isn’t about how “perfect” your hair looks; it comes from consistent and proper care. Although polished hairstyles can look more organized and put together, they aren't the best choice for maintaining healthy hair. By choosing hairstyles that prioritize your hair’s health rather than appearance and following trends, you’re able to achieve healthier, stronger, and better results overall for your hair.
